About the Project

This project focuses on analyzing the Algerian Forest Fires dataset to understand the relationship between climate conditions and forest fire risks. Utilizing Ridge Regression, we aim to predict the Fire Weather Index (FWI) based on various meteorological and environmental factors.

Dataset Description

The dataset contains observations from the Algerian forest regions, recording key weather parameters and fire risk indices over multiple months. It includes features such as temperature, relative humidity, wind speed, rainfall, and various fire weather indices like FFMC, DMC, DC, ISI, BUI, and FWI.

Research Problem

The study aims to predict fire risk based on environmental variables and understand the impact of climate conditions on forest ecosystems in regions prone to wildfires. By analyzing forest fire occurrences in Algeria, we seek to offer insights into how climate change and extreme weather patterns affect wildfire activity globally.

Regression Techniques Used

During our analysis, we explored various regression techniques to find the most effective model for predicting the Fire Weather Index (FWI). After comparing the performance of different models, we settled on using Ridge Regression for the deployed model due to its superior performance.

  • Ridge Regression: Selected as the final model, Ridge Regression provided the best balance between bias and variance, effectively handling multicollinearity among predictors. It achieved a high R2 score of 0.984 and a low Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 0.564.
  • ElasticNet Regression: While ElasticNet was considered, it did not perform as well as Ridge Regression for our dataset, with a lower R2 score and higher MAE.

Model Performance

The Ridge Regression model demonstrated excellent predictive capabilities:

  • Mean Absolute Error (MAE): 0.564
  • R2 Score: 0.984

These metrics indicate that the model accurately predicts the Fire Weather Index, making it a reliable tool for assessing fire risk.

Expected Outcomes

- Develop a robust predictive model to forecast fire risk based on climate conditions.
- Identify key environmental factors influencing forest fire risks.
- Provide insights for climate change research and inform forest management policies.

Conclusion

Through rigorous analysis and model evaluation, we concluded that Ridge Regression offers the best performance for predicting the Fire Weather Index in our dataset. This deployed model can significantly aid in early detection and prevention strategies for forest fires, contributing valuable insights to climate change research and environmental conservation efforts.
